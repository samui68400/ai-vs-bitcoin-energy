Why these numbers are reliable

Bitcoin — measured, not estimated ■ high confidence

Bitcoin is the only energy system in the world whose total activity is public down to the second. The hashrate (global computing power) can be read directly from the blockchain via difficulty and block timing.

P (GW) = Hashrate (EH/s) × Fleet efficiency (J/TH) × PUE ÷ 1000

This page reads the hashrate live and applies the average fleet efficiency (~16.5 J/TH) and a PUE of 1.10, calibrated to reproduce the central estimate of the Cambridge CBECI — the academic reference cited by the IEA and the White House. Model error margin: roughly ±15%.

AI — the best available proxy ■ medium confidence

No hashrate exists for AI: hyperscalers do not publish their consumption in real time. The only honest approach is a nowcast: daily interpolation between solid annual anchor points.

TWh(t) = interpolation( IEA/CBECI anchors 2020→2030 )(t)

Anchors: ~110 TWh (2024), ~190 TWh (2025, ≈40% of the 485 TWh consumed by data centers), IEA trajectory toward a tripling by 2030. The uncertainty band (±25%) spans the gap between published models (IEA, SemiAnalysis, de Vries/Digiconomist). This is exactly how central banks handle unobservable data: anchor, bound, display the uncertainty.
